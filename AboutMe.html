Bio

Hello, my name is Dario, I am an Italian second-year computer science student at Queen Mary University of London. My biggest passion, as you might guess, are computers. A passion that I grew over the years.

It all started from playing videogames, my first ever game I played was Rayman 3, I really loved that game so much that Rayman became one of my favourite “superhero” for many years. But while I was gaming I always had this question in mind: “How is this piece of metal doing this?"
So curious that I took one apart, lucky enough I didn’t break it. Then, I started looking for a way to study it in a “proper way” and picked computer science as my GCSE, then for my A-Levels, and ultimately as my University course.

Computer science was not the only thing I loved. While I was studying A-Level physics I discovered the topic of quantum physics and because of my love for computer science with some research and with the help of my physics teacher I discovered quantum computers. I do believe that this is going to be the future of computers and I would love to be part of that.

My biggest dream which isn’t computer science related is to create a time machine; I really hope it is going to work thanks to a computer, so at least I can work and play with it.
