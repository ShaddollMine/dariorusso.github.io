3D Rotiating Cube

After programming a couple of 2D games, I started thinking about how I could program 3D games. I started looking on youtube, and I realized that most good quality videos about 3D graphics explained the topic in C++.
However, that was not enough to stop me, and this is how I started watching a video from a youtube channel called: javidx9. He explained the topic well by programming a graphic engine from scratch rather than using an existing one. This way of teaching made me understand well the concepts and mathematics involved behind Graphic engines.

How matrices are used to program in A 3D space is the concept I liked the most. In my opinion, the matrix that got emphasized the most through the youtube course I followed is the projection matrix. The projection matrix is a matrix that transforms a series of 3D coordinates in a way that they can be visualized in a 2D space. This is performed by multiplying the 3D coordinates with this matrix. The matrix itself is a combination of concepts that takes in count how wide we can see, the distance of the object from the origin, and more.

Finally, I took these concepts into practice and translated the examples javidx9 gave from C++ into Java. And this is how I ended up with a 3D cube.

However, it was somewhat boring, because the cube was static, and couldn't really tell the difference with a 2D cube. So kept following the video and learned about other matrices used to translate, rotate and do every sort of movement with 3D coordinate. So I implemented 2 rotations, one on the x-axis and one on the y-axis, making sure that the rotation was asynchronous, ending with this marvelous effect.

I particularly enjoyed this project and I hope that in the future I will have some free time to expand my knowledge about 3D graphics and how 3D games are implemented.
